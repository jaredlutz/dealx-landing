Private markets reward patience, but they do not reward passivity. The difference is discipline: clear underwriting standards, documented assumptions, and a portfolio view that survives leadership changes and market shifts.

## Structure before story

In public markets, price discovery is continuous. In private markets, **terms, covenants, and cash-flow mechanics** often matter as much as the headline yield. Teams that start from structure—what must be true for the investment to work—tend to make fewer emotional decisions when conditions tighten.

## A portfolio lens

Single-name diligence is necessary but not sufficient. Institutional allocators typically ask how a position fits concentration limits, liquidity budgets, and the rest of the book. The same lens applies whether you are evaluating real-estate-backed credit, sponsor-backed structures, or other private sleeves.
